Reclaiming the scholarly social web

Who owns your research infrastructure?

The nearly overnight collapse of 'Science Twitter' in 2022 destroyed a fantastic, interdisciplinary scholarly network born from many years of community engagement. This disruption exposed a vulnerability many had overlooked: scholarly communication built on corporate platforms is fragile by design. Enshittification, data colonialism, and hostile acquisitions are not edge cases – they are features of the system.

The lesson was clear: we can't afford to outsource scientific discourse to private corporations. Yet most of our digital infrastructure, including social media, code sharing and collaboration tools, still belongs to a handful of tech giants.

archaeo.social & archaeo.dev are different

We are a community-run, open-source collective providing decentralised digital services built by archaeologists, for archaeologists. Our infrastructure is resilient, transparent, and collectively owned, with hundreds of users and several scholarly societies drawing value from it and providing support.

Pooling resources across our community makes this sustainable. We invite CAA members to use our services – and help shape their future!

The Fediverse

Mastodon is a free and open-source software platform for decentralized social networking and microblogging. It is one of the main alternative to Twitter/X. archaeo.social is a Mastodon instance for archaeologists, ancient historians and anyone interested in scholarly communication about the human past.

Join at: archaeo.social

Matrix Chat

Matrix is a free and open-source, decentralized, instant-messaging platform. It is one of the main alternatives to Whatsapp or Slack, with a focus on user privacy and encryption. Communities such as Young Researchers in Archaeometry are already present on our matrix server.

Join at: chat.archaeo.social

Git Forge

Forgejo is a git forge and a software development platform. It is a free and open-source, collectively-governed, alternative to GitHub. Forgejo provides a features-packed collaboration platform for developers with an intuitive and familiar UI. A FOSS platform is the idea companion to Open Science.

Join at: forge.archaeo.dev

Webring

If your eyes landed on this section first, congrats! you are old enough to remember the web 1.0. Webrings were a way to connect websites that shared a similar theme. This webring can also help in discovering new archaeology blogs around the web. Come join us, we have some hand-crafted old-style buttons too!

Join at: webring.archaeo.social

Collaborative Editing

Hedgedoc is an open-source, web-based, collaborative note-taking editor in Markdown format and a lightweight alternative to Google Docs. Hedgedoc can be used to easily collaborate with others on notes, graphs, and even presentations, all in real time.

Join at: md.archaeo.dev
